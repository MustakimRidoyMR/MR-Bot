Can you explain quantum computing in simple terms?

Imagine regular computers like a light switch - they use bits that are either ON (1) or OFF (0). Quantum computers use quantum bits or "qubits" that can be both ON and OFF at the same time (called superposition) - like a light switch that's both up and down simultaneously. This allows quantum computers to process massive amounts of possibilities all at once, solving certain problems much faster than regular computers.
